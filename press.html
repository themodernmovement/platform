About the Network

The Modern Movement Network is a nonpartisan, volunteer-run civic technology platform with three connected properties:

  • YouCanSayNo.org — public opposition platform exposing harmful corporate projects with sourced pollution metrics, corporate accountability rankings, and community voting
  • ICanSayYes.org — community proposal and voting platform where citizens propose real, specific measures for governing bodies to act on
  • TheModernMovement.org — the member hub, admin platform, and community backbone

We accept no corporate funding. We run no advertising. We are powered by Supabase (open-source database), Vercel (hosting), and the conviction that communities have the right to fight back.

Active Issues We Are Tracking

  • Benson, AZ Aluminum Smelter — Aluminum Dynamics, Inc. (Mississippi) proposes a 174,000 sq ft aluminum recycling facility on the San Pedro River. ADEQ air quality permit issued December 2025. Emits PFCs and hydrogen chloride exceeding major source threshold. 388 gal/min groundwater draw. Less than 0.5 miles from a school. Health Over Wealth Benson lawsuit filed September 2025. Arizona AG Kris Mayes considering public nuisance action.
  • Project Jupiter — Doña Ana County, NM — OpenAI and Oracle backing $165B in public bonds to build a massive AI data center in Santa Teresa, NM. Powered by twin private gas-fired microgrid plants. Combined GHG equal to Albuquerque + Las Cruces annually. Permit split across two separate applications to dodge state review thresholds. 7,155 public comments filed. Hearing scheduled July 21, 2026.
  • NM Credit Union Watch — Fifteen New Mexico federally tax-exempt credit unions funneled member deposits into secret executive retirement plans (SERPs) via Bank-Owned Life Insurance. Artesia CU: 24.2% of total equity. State Employees CU: $25.8M. Zero member votes held. Zero public disclosure. NCUA public filings sourced.

About The Modern Movement Network

The Modern Movement Network is a nonpartisan, volunteer-operated civic technology platform. Its properties — YouCanSayNo.org, ICanSayYes.org, and TheModernMovement.org — provide communities with tools to oppose harmful corporate projects, vote on community measures, and hold institutions accountable. The Network accepts no corporate funding, runs no advertising, and does not sell user data. All issue data is sourced to public records, permit filings, or investigative journalism. The Network was founded on the belief that enough ordinary people, organized around a clear and verifiable message, can still change things.
