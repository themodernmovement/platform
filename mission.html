// About the Modern Movement Network

We Fight for the People Corporations Forgot.

The Modern Movement is a nonpartisan civic network built on one belief: that communities have the right to know what is being done to their air, water, land, and money — and the power to say no to it.

Why We Exist

Billion-dollar corporations and opaque institutions have learned to move fast and count on communities moving slow. They pick low-income towns. They split permits to dodge review. They fund executive retirements with member deposits. They build gas plants in communities that asked for clean energy. They do it because they can — because the political and legal machinery favors capital over people.

We exist to change that math. Not with money. With organized, verifiable, public community voice.

Our Platforms

✅

ICanSayYes.org

A community voting platform where ordinary people propose, fact-check, and vote on real, specific measures that can be adopted by city councils, legislatures, and governing bodies.

❌

YouCanSayNo.org

A public accountability platform exposing harmful corporate projects — aluminum smelters, dirty gas data centers, secret executive benefit schemes — and organizing community opposition with verifiable data.

What We Stand For

  • Transparency: every vote is public, every claim is sourced, every corporate backer is named
  • Nonpartisanship: clean water, safe air, and honest institutions are not left or right — they are human
  • Accountability: if a governing body ignores a certified community vote, that fact is logged and published permanently
  • Access: no account required to vote, no paywall, no ads, no corporate funding
  • Locality: we fight the specific fights in the specific communities where they happen

What We Will Never Do

  • Accept corporate funding or advertising revenue
  • Sell or share your personal data
  • Publish unverified claims — all issues are sourced to public records, permit filings, or investigative journalism
  • Take partisan political positions — we are pro-community, not pro-party
  • Stay quiet when institutions ignore the people they claim to serve

The Unifying Thread

Whether it is a Mississippi metal company building an aluminum smelter on the last free-flowing river in the American Southwest, OpenAI and Oracle financing gas plants in a community that asked for clean energy, or New Mexico credit union boards quietly funneling member deposits into secret executive retirement accounts — the pattern is identical:

Corporations and institutions exploiting communities with no accountability, no transparency, and no benefit to the people they claim to serve.

We track them. We name them. We amplify the community voices pushing back. We do not stop.
